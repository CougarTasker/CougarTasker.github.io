Minesweeper AI

Here is an AI for solving minesweeper that I made based on what i have learnt in my CS2910 AI module about symbolic AI. This JS implemnntation was based on a FOL prolog psuedocode where for all the unknown cells adjacent to a known square are grouped together into a range. if a range is full or empty we can be certain of its cells value. expanding this knowledge base is done by removing known elements from from ranges to create smaller ones and splitting a range in two where one is a subset of another. I havent proven this to be correct but intuativly it seems right. occasionally there isn't enough data to deduce the correct value and at this point the AI will take a guess. in a future version this guess can be wieghted towards the cells that are most probable but at the moment it is not.

press the step button to move forward one step in the cycle, this is resolving one certain range and marking the relevent squares or making a guess if it has too.

the AI has no inside knowledge of the actual arrangments of the mines it knows as much as you can see, that said it will retry the first move untill it succeeds otherwise there is about 11% chance of it failing of the first go

you can also make moved manually if you would just like to play the game or see how the AI will react to different choices
